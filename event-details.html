3:00 PM - Until something explodes
The Main Auditorium (and surrounding blast radius)

The 42nd Annual Science Exhibition

Join us for a celebration of science, innovation, and the indomitable spirit of students who definitely started their projects last night. This year's theme is "Volcanoes, Robots, and Questionable Life Choices." Safety goggles will be provided and are absolutely not optional.

Witness the marvels of modern student engineering, from potato-powered clocks to robots designed to do laundry (they mostly just knock things over). It's a day of fun, learning, and minor, controlled chaos.

Event Highlights

  • A baking soda volcano that we're 90% sure won't erupt this year.
  • Guest lecture: "Why Pluto Had It Coming" by a very passionate astronomer.
  • A robotics competition where the last robot standing wins.
  • Live demonstrations of things that probably shouldn't be mixed together.
  • Free pizza for anyone who can explain quantum physics in 30 seconds or less.

5:45 PM - 6:00 PM
