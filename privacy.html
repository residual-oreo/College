Our Not-So-Secret Privacy Policy

Hereâ€™s the deal. You give us your data. We try not to lose it. Fair?

1. Information We "Borrow"

We collect information to make your experience better and to figure out how many pizzas to order for student events. This includes:

  • Your Identity: Your name, student ID, and the alias you use for online gaming. We respect "DragonSlayer42."
  • Academic Performance: Your grades, attendance, and how many times you've fallen asleep in an 8 AM lecture.
  • Digital Footprints: The Wi-Fi networks you connect to, especially if you're trying to stream movies during a seminar. We see you.

2. How We Use Your Precious Data

Your information is vital to our operations. We use it for highly important tasks, such as:

  • Powering the Dean's coffee machine with predictive analytics.
  • Training our campus squirrels to deliver mail (results are mixed).
  • Creating charts that prove students are more productive when there are free donuts in the library.
  • Personalizing your experience by reminding you of that assignment you haven't started yet.

3. Who We Share Your Secrets With

We guard your data like it's the last slice of pizza. We only share it under specific, dire circumstances:

  • With Your Permission: If you explicitly ask us to tell your parents you're "studying hard."
  • For Legal Reasons: If government agents with very serious sunglasses show up and ask for it.
  • The Squirrels: They need your class schedule for their delivery routes. It's complicated.

4. Our Top-Secret Security Measures

We protect your data with a complex system of state-of-the-art security protocols, including:

  • An encrypted database protected by a password that is definitely not "password123."
  • A very intimidating-looking server rack with lots of blinking lights.
  • An intern named Dave who has been told not to click on suspicious links.

5. Your Rights (You Have Them!)

You're in control. Mostly. You have the right to:

  • Ask to see the data we have on you (prepare to be underwhelmed).
  • Correct any information, like changing your major from Philosophy to "Professional Napper."
  • Request we delete your data, which we'll do right after we finish one last donut-related study.
